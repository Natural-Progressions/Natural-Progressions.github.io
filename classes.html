Acharacter’s class is most likely the part that most greatly impacts a build. Unlike other TTRPGs, Natural Progressions doesn’t have the concept of subclasses. Instead, each class is split into it’s implementations of two archetypes, martial and magic.

Despite seemingly being opposites of each other, the two archetypes for each class are brought together by the overarching theme of each class. For example, the Novitiate class is built around the concept of an individual who studies and trains within existing institutions, and the class features of the Fighter and Wizard are built around the same idea.

The beauty of having each class implement both archetypes is that no multiclassing is necessary to have a character that has abilities from the martial and magic archetypes. As a character levels up, they can choose which archetype of their class they want to take a level in. The only limitation to this switching is that the archetype you chose at Level 1 is greater than or equal to the level of the opposite.

For example, when a level 1 Novitiate with 1 level in Fighter, levels up to Level 2, they can choose (assuming no multiclassing) to take the second level Fighter feature, or the first level Wizard feature. Assuming they took the first level Wizard feature, when they level up to level 3 (with the same assumption of no multiclassing), they would have to take the second level Fighter feature.

General Sections

Each class will start off with a description of the overarching idea behind it. Following this description will come the two archetypes, first the might implementation, then the magic.

Bonuses, Skills and Stats

Skill Modifier Bonuses: The skills that have their modifier increases will be detailed here. Generally, there will be options, such as “choose 3 of the following skills”. There is no overlap when a character takes levels in both archetypes.

Weapon Modifier Bonuses: The weapons groups that a character has an increased weapon modifier for. There is no overlap when a character takes levels in both archetypes.

Armor Training: The armor types that the archetype can use. The training for light and medium armor overlaps - if the might or magic archetype is trained in light or medium armor and you have a level in each archetype, you are trained in it. For heavy armor, your initial archetype must be trained in it.

Shield Training: Whether you can use shields. This training overlaps - if either archetype has training and you have a level in both, you are trained in Shields.

Starting Equipment: Rather self explanatory. There obviously is no overlap when a character takes levels in both archetypes.

Many systems end up with a divide of their player-classes being spellcasters, or completely martial. Natural Progressions has instead gone down a path that allows players to easily mix and match this types with the delineation of archetypes within each class. A natural extension of this split is to tie features and abilities to number of levels within each archetype, regardless of what class the levels are in.

Spellcasting Specialization

With two levels in Martial archetypes, your training with a specific way of fighting starts to show. Choose one fighting style from the following options:

Ambush: You have learned that striking hard and fast while hidden is the fastest way to victory. When you attack an opponent that does not know you are present, you deal an extra 1d6 damage from your weapon.

Archery: You are a skilled and focused marksman. You gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls to all ranged weapons.

Blind Fighting: Choose another sense - it should make sense with your race and culture. With your chosen sense, you have honed ability to use this sense in close quarters. You gain Blindsight up to 10 ft while you can use this sense. Within this range, you can see any creature that has not successfully hidden from you.

Defense: You adopt a fighting style focused on defense. Your weapon bonus to your armor class increases by +1 while you are wielding a melee weapon.

Dual Wielding: You have been trained to use multiple weapons at once. You gain a +1 attack bonus to all double-ended weapons, and when wielding multiple weapons.

Dueling: You have been trained to fight prolonged duels. While you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand, and no other weapons in your other hand, you gain +1 bonus to attack rolls with that weapon.

Great Weapon Fighting: You have been trained to utilize the large, sweeping strikes of larger weapons. When using a weapon with 2 hands, you gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls with that weapon.

Thrown Weapon Fighting: You have been trained in the art of precise weapon throwing. You gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls with thrown weapons.

Unarmed Fighting: You have been trained in martial arts. You gain a +1 attack bonus to your unarmed strikes.

With two levels in Magic archetypes, your training with a specific way type of magic starts to show. Choose one School of Magic Specialization from the following options:

Abjuration: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Abjuration spells. When casting Abjuration spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Abjuration Spellcasting Modifier.

Conjuration: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Conjuration spells. When casting Conjuration spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Conjuration Spellcasting Modifier.

Divination: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Divination spells. When casting Divination spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Divination Spellcasting Modifier.

Enchantment: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Enchantment spells. When casting Enchantment spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Enchantment Spellcasting Modifier.

Evocation: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Evocation spells. When casting Evocation spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Evocation Spellcasting Modifier.

Illusion: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Illusion spells. When casting Illusion spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Illusion Spellcasting Modifier.

Malediction: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Malediction spells. When casting Malediction spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Malediction Spellcasting Modifier.

Necromancy: You have been trained with an emphasis on casting Necromancy spells. When casting Necromancy spells, you gain a +1 bonus to your Necromancy Spellcasting Modifier.

As hinted in the chapter overview, having a character multiclass is possible. However, there are a few restrictions.

In order to multiclass into another class’s archetype that matches your primary one, you must have more levels in your primary class than what you are multiclassing into. If you are a level 1 Novitiate with one level in Fighter, you can multiclass into any other class’ martial archetype, such as Artisan’s Craftsmen. When leveling up to level 3, assuming you took the level 1 Craftsmen feature as your level 2 bonus, you can multiclass into another class’ martial archetype that is not Artisan, or you can take the second level Fighter feature.

You cannot take the level two Craftsmen feature, because then your number of levels in Craftsmen would outweight your number of levels in Fighter.

It is not considered multiclassing to take levels in your class’s opposite archetype - a level 1 Novitiate with one level in Fighter can take their level 2 increase in Wizard. However, with subsequent level ups, you must take a second level in Fighter prior to levelling up Wizard. Your number of levels in your primary archetype must be equal to or greater than the number of levels in the other.

Finally, when multiclassing into another class’ opposite archetype, your primary class’ opposite archetype must be at an equal or higher level than the second class’. For instance, say that a character started off as a level 1 Novitiate (Fighter). They could not multiclass into Artisan (Artificier) until level 4.

Novitiate (Fighter) 1 + Artisan (Artificier) 1 doesn’t work as the character’s Novitiate (Wizard) level is not equal to or greater than Artisan (Artificier) 1.

Novitiate (Fighter) 1 + Novitiate (Wizard) 1 + Artisan (Artificier) 1 doesn’t work as the character’s Magic archetype’s level is greater than the character’s Martial archetype (which is their primary).

This character would need to be Novitiate (Fighter) 2 + Novitiate (Wizard) 1 in order to take a level in Artisan (Artificier).

Features Gained

When you first multiclass into another class or take the first level in your classes’ opposite Archetype , you do not gain any of the following modifiers:

  • Skill Modifier Bonuses
  • Tool Modifier Bonuses
  • Weapon Modifier Bonuses
  • Spellcasting Modifier Bonuses
  • Starting Equipment

For Armor Training, you only gain training with Light Armor and Medium Armor if the class you are multiclassing into has it. If the class you are multiclassing into has it, you gain training with Shields.

You gain the amount of mana and spells as your class's magic archetype implementation should you be multiclassing into another class's magic archetype. You still use your original Primary Spellcasting Modifier rather than the new class'.
