Like weapons and skills, each type of toolkit has its own separate equipment modifier, to a maximum of +11 to your 1d20 roll. This does mean that at higher equipment modifiers, your character may start to auto-pass certain activies with a tool kit. In such situations, at the discretion of your LM, you may not roll for those activities (with an auto-pass).

Normally when making a check with a tool, you roll 1d20 and add your tool modifier (just like skills and weapons). However, when using a tool in conjunction with a skill check, your roll is 1d20 + your skill modifier + your tool modifier. If you roll a natural 20, both the tool and the skill get increased.

At note for LMs: Each tool has a set of skills that they buff and can be used with. If a player is attempting to do an unlisted combination of tool + skill (for the combined check), it is recommended to allow it so long as they provide a logical argument.

Likewise, if they would like to attempt another activity not listed using a tool set, if you believe it's a feasible thing to try, it is recommend to allow them to roll.
