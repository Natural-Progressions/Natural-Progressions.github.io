In Natural Progressions, weapons and tools function just like skills - the modifier used is increased on a roll of a natural 20 and they have a maximum of 11, assuming no variant rules are used.

Weapons are grouped by categories. For example, when rolling a natural 20 when wielding a dagger, your Dueling Blades modifier increases by 1.

To give the feeling of progressing as a character gets better with a weapon, the traits and damage of weapons change depending on your weapon modifier - this is unique to each weapon (although bonuses may be given to a group of weapons, such as all Ambush Weapons). The ability levels are as follows:

Modified Range Training Level
0-1 Untrained
2-4 Trained
5-8 Experienced
9-10 Expert
11 Master

Experienced, Expert and Master all have level requirements in order to use. A characterâ€™s modifier can increase up to 11 without hitting the requirements, but their ability level will remain at Trained unless they hit the requirements.

Experienced: 2 levels in any Martial archetype. These levels can be split in any way among the Martial archetypes.

Expert: 5 levels in any Martial archetype. 2 of these levels must be within the same archetype.

Master: 11 levels in any Martial archetype. 5 of these levels must be within the same archetype.

Additionally, some abilities and properties granted by weapons have a weapon DC, which is 10 + your weapon ability modifier.

Improvised Weapons

Theoretically, just about anything can be considered a weapon with enough force - a rock, a bottle, even a pin. However, they generally are not nearly as effective as actual weapons.

With no abilities and features that affect improvised weapons, you use your Athletics modifier when determining if the attack hits. Upon a successful attack, you deal 1 damage of a type that makes sense. Generally it will be bludgeoning damage.

Each combination of weapon and ability level has a set of properties that define what a weapon can and cannot do.

The number of weapons can make choosing a starting weapon(s) daunting. For games where players wish to have a reduced number of weapons to choose from, the following alternate choices are suggested:
