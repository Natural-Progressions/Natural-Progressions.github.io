Armor

Armors directly affect your characterâ€™s Armor Class, and generally is the second biggest modifier behind your reflex skill modifier. As stated above, your Armor Class is calculated as follows:

10 + armor bonus + weapon bonus + shield bonus + reflex skill modifier

Unlike weapons and skills, armors do not have a skill modifier that increase with a natural 20. The reflex skill modifier fulfills this role for the Armor Class calculation.

However, like weapons, there are different types of armors: light armor, medium armor, and heavy armor. In order to use any of these types of armor, you need to be trained in them - a character is either trained or untrained in each type of armor. This training status is gotten by your Race/Culture, Background and Class.

A character wearing armor they are not trained in gains no effects of the armor (armor bonus in terms of the character's AC calculation will be 0), and may have other effects (as determined by other rules).

Additionally, your type of armor can affect your stealth skill - some armors give disadvantage to stealth checks while being worn.

Shields

Like armor, shields are a part of the Armor Class calculation and do not have an ability modifier associated with them - characters are trained or untrained with Shields.

Unlike armor, characters still get the AC Bonus associated with each shield if they are untrained.

For every shield, there is a time to don, and that can be different depending if the shield is held or strapped. Shields that are strapped cannot be disarmed, however, a strapped shield is considered using the shield with one hand. This does mean that in order to perform an activity that requires two hands, such as wielding a Great Sword, you would need to doff any shield you are using.
